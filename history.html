The great history of women in coding




  • 1840s

    Ada Lovelace

    Way back in the 19th Century, Ada made the ground-breaking observation that early computers could be used for more than pure mathmatics. Ada wrote the first algorithm to be carried out by a computer and in so doing became the first computer programmer.

  • 1940s

    Kilogirls

    Throughout World War II, women played an important role in the development of modern computing to support the war effort. Laborious calculations and computing were seen as "women's work" and the term "kilogirl" was coined to describe the women who worked as human computers.

  • 1960s

    Margaret Hamilton

    In 1965, Margaret was responsible for programming the software for the onboard flight software on the Apollo mission computers.

  • 1970s

    Adele Goldberg

    Adele played a crucial role in the team that developed Smalltalk, one of the first object-oriented programming languages. Smalltalk was instrumental in the design of Apple's first Macintosh computer.
